About Me

This is who I am.

I am a PhD student at the University of New Hampshire, Durham, USA. I am part of the Text Retrieval Extraction Machine learning and Analysis (TREMA) lab at UNH. I work with Dr. Laura Dietz. I am interested in entity retrieval and integrating passage and entity features to find better entities for a given query. I am also interested in graph based retrievals , probabilistic graphical models, question answering and applications of machine learning to text. My current work is related to the Support Passage Retrieval for Complex Answer Retrieval (CAR) task at the Text Retrieval Conference (TREC). Given a ranking of entities relevant for a query, I want to find a paragraph which explains to the user, why this entity is relevant to the paragraph.

Prior to this, I received my Master in Science (MSc) degree in Computer Science from St. Xavier's College, Kolkata, India, an autonomous institution under the University of Calcutta. During my masters, I worked on Multi-Objective Optimization using Genetic Algorithms. Before, I received my Bachelor in Science (BSc) in Computer Science from the same institution and worked on Object Detection and Tracking in Videos.

I am a voracious reader and my favourite genre is detective and mystery. I think I have read (almost) all the works of Agatha Christie and Sir Arthur Conan Doyle. I am very interested in Hindu mythology and if you are too, you might want to read books by Devdutt Pattanaik. I also like to read about world history occasionally. Apart from this, I am also a trained Indian Classical vocalist and a pianist and perform regularly at various events organised by the Indian community in New Hampshire and Massachusetts. I love to travel.
